# Part 7: Quality of Service

This section covers QoS: the mechanisms that allow a network to treat different types of traffic differently. Without QoS, a network delivers all packets on a best-effort basis. With QoS, voice and video traffic can be prioritised over bulk data transfers, ensuring that delay-sensitive applications remain usable even when links are congested.

Topics covered include traffic classification and marking (DSCP and CoS), queuing mechanisms (FIFO, WFQ, LLQ, CBWFQ), and traffic policing versus shaping.

# Professional Standards & Frameworks - Domain Overview

> **This domain is awaiting contributors.** Open a GitHub Issue with label `professional-standards`.

## The Analogy

Think of professional standards as the **building codes and electrical regulations** that govern construction. A builder can put up a house without knowing them - but that house may not be safe, may not be insured, and may not pass inspection. Engineers who know the codes make better decisions automatically, because the codes encode decades of hard lessons.

Standards in networking are the same: you can run a network without knowing NIST SP 800-53 or BCP38, but when something goes wrong - a security breach, a capacity failure, a regulatory audit - the engineer who knows the standards will know where to look and what questions to ask.

---

## Why This Domain Exists

Most networking curricula teach technology. Very few teach the **frameworks that professional engineers are expected to operate within**. When you move from junior to senior roles, you will encounter:

- **Security audits** that reference NIST and ISO 27001
- **SLA commitments** defined by MEF standards
- **Data centre design reviews** against TIA-942 tiers
- **Incident response procedures** shaped by ITIL
- **Network hygiene expectations** like BCP38 anti-spoofing

Knowing these frameworks does not replace technical skill - it gives your technical decisions a defensible basis and helps you communicate with management, customers, and auditors.

# Contributing to the Network Living Compendium

Thank you for your interest in contributing.

This project is a **Network Living Compendium**: a continuously evolving, network-curated body of knowledge designed to compound over time.

We welcome contributions from humans, teams, and AI-assisted workflows.

---

## 1. What We Accept

We welcome contributions that improve the **clarity, accuracy, usefulness, and interconnectedness** of the compendium, including:

### Knowledge Contributions
- New concepts, principles, or definitions
- Patterns and playbooks derived from real-world practice
- Clarifications, corrections, and refinements
- Synthesis across existing content
- References, citations, or supporting evidence

### Tooling Contributions (Optional)
- Automation for ingestion, synthesis, or validation
- Scripts, agents, or workflows that support the compendium
- Visualizations or analysis tools

---

## 2. Contribution Philosophy

Please align with the following principles:

- **Signal over noise** — fewer, higher-quality contributions are preferred
- **Explain context, not just conclusions**
- **Prefer synthesis over raw accumulation**
- **Make relationships explicit** (links, references, dependencies)
- **Assume future readers do not share your context**

This is not a note dump or personal wiki.
It is curated, evolving collective intelligence.

---

## 3. AI‑Assisted Contributions

AI assistance is explicitly allowed and encouraged.

However:
- Contributors remain responsible for accuracy
- AI-generated content must be reviewed before submission
- Hallucinated facts are treated as errors

If a contribution is substantially AI-generated, please note this in the pull request description.
Contributors must ensure that AI‑assisted content does not reproduce copyrighted material beyond what is permitted by the original license.

---

## 4. Licensing of Contributions

By submitting a contribution, you agree that:
- Knowledge and documentation contributions are licensed under  **CC BY-SA 4.0**
- Code contributions are licensed under **Apache License 2.0**
- You have the right to submit the contribution under these terms

This ensures the compendium remains open, reusable, and legally coherent.
If you cannot agree to these terms, please do not submit a contribution.

Attribution requirements for sourced or adapted material are described in Section 7.

---

## 5. How to Contribute

### Step 1: Fork the Repository
Create your own fork on GitHub.

### Step 2: Make Focused Changes
- One concept or improvement per PR is preferred
- Keep commits small and well described

### Step 3: Open a Pull Request
In your PR description, please include:
- What changed
- Why it improves the compendium
- Any relevant references or evidence
- Whether AI assistance was used

---

## 6. Review Process

Maintainers may:
- Ask for clarification or evidence
- Suggest restructuring or synthesis
- Edit for clarity, coherence, or consistency
- Decline contributions that do not align with the project’s goals

This is curation, not censorship.

---

## 7. Code of Conduct

All contributors are expected to be:
- Respectful
- Professional
- Focused on ideas, not individuals
Harassment, discrimination, or bad-faith behavior will not be tolerated.

Contributors must clearly attribute all sourced or adapted material to its original creator(s) and original license.
You may only contribute content that:
- You created yourself, or
- Is compatible with CC BY‑SA 4.0 and correctly attributed
Third‑party material remains under its original license and is not relicensed simply by inclusion in this compendium.

By contributing, you confirm that at least one of the following is true:
- You are the original author, or
- The material is used in accordance with its original license
And that, in all cases:
- All required attribution is provided

---

## 8. Governance & Stewardship

Maintainers act as **stewards**, not owners.
Their role is to:
- Preserve coherence
- Prevent entropy
- Ensure long-term compounding value

Decisions are guided by:
- Alignment with the project’s purpose
- Long-term usefulness
- Quality of reasoning and evidence

---

## 9. Final Note

Small improvements matter.
Clear explanations matter.
Connections matter most.

Thank you for helping this compendium stay alive.
